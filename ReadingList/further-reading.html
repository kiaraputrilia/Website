Okay, now! Before you peruse through my reading list, there is one that I'd love for you to read. (Clicking on the title starts an automatic download) It's my annotated copy of Like Someone in Love An Addendum to Love Song by Masha Tupitsyn. My professor gave this out as an assigned reading back in February 2023, but I haven't stopped thinking about it! You can definitely skip this part of me rambling (but if you love hearing me ramble some more then do check out my website) about it but here are my two cents on it: In the past few months, I have really taken a liking to the nature of websites and their endless hyperlinks—jumping from one platform to another, from text to video, from song to text, from text to more text, (and the list of possibilities goes on). It’s as if we’re constantly traversing from one stream of thought to another, where everything is interwoven. The reading experience of Like Someone in Love: An Addendum to Love Dog, felt exactly just like that. But beyond that, we also feel like this great passage of time. The reading is interestingly structured into two columns which sometimes become a space for juxtaposing her own retelling of her life and other outside references of text, sound, and images. As I was reading through it, I found myself treating her text as hyperlinks—highlighting passages I love and highlighting names of her other references that she incorporated into her text. This document turned into multiple browser windows. And yet, It feels like a handwritten digital archive, (though I know both those words together are antithetical to each other). Masha’s inclusion of other people’s words is imbued into her own, almost like she then started to “own” them too, which I think is the case for a lot of us when we jot down quotes and bookmark images. Perhaps, other people’s words can better explain our feelings—Masha best explained this on page 35; her obsession with words and seeking the truth from them. The differing quantitive entries are reflective of her thoughts, where sometimes she simply includes a quote and/or an image that felt sufficient enough to understand how she was feeling that day.
